VAE Latent Space
Compression Before Diffusion

How the Variational Autoencoder compresses 786,432 pixels into 16,384 latent values — making Stable Diffusion possible on consumer GPUs.

8× Spatial Compression 786k → 16k Values KL-Regularised
01

Why Compress at All?

Running diffusion directly on 512×512 pixels would mean denoising 786,432 values at every step. The VAE shrinks this 48× before diffusion ever starts.

Spatial Compression: 512 → 64

Four successive stride-2 convolutional layers each halve the spatial dimensions: 512 → 256 → 128 → 64. The channel dimension simultaneously expands from 3 (RGB) to 4 latent channels, giving the model more expressive capacity at the compact resolution.

Dimension flow through VAE encoder $(3, 512, 512) \to (4, 64, 64)$
786,432 values → 16,384 values (48× reduction)
02

The Encoder & Reparameterisation

The encoder does not map each image to a single point. It maps to a Gaussian: a mean μ and standard deviation σ — then samples a latent vector from that distribution.

Encoding: Image → Distribution

For each input image x, the encoder produces two vectors of equal size: the mean μ and the log-variance log σ². These define a Gaussian distribution over possible latent codes — not a single deterministic point.

Encoder output $E(x) \to \mu,\; \log \sigma^2 \quad (\text{each of shape } 4 \times 64 \times 64)$

Reparameterisation Trick

Sampling from a distribution is non-differentiable — gradients cannot flow through a random sample. The reparameterisation trick moves the randomness outside the computation graph, making the sample differentiable with respect to μ and σ.

Reparameterisation (ε ∼ N(0,1) is fixed noise) $z = \mu + \varepsilon \cdot \sigma$

Now gradients flow through μ and σ during backpropagation because ε is just a constant noise vector — not a learnable parameter.

KL Regularisation: Keeping the Latent Space Smooth

A second term in the VAE loss function penalises the encoder's distribution for drifting too far from a standard Gaussian N(0, I). This KL divergence term prevents the latent space from collapsing into isolated islands and ensures that every point in latent space decodes to a plausible image.

VAE Training Objective (ELBO) $\mathcal{L} = \mathbb{E}[\log p(x|z)] - \beta \cdot \mathrm{KL}(q(z|x) \| \mathcal{N}(0,I))$
Why this matters for diffusion
Without KL regularisation, the latent space would have large empty gaps between clusters. Stable Diffusion starts from Gaussian noise z ~ N(0, I) — KL regularisation guarantees that this random starting point lands somewhere meaningful that the decoder can reconstruct.

The Decoder: Called Only Once

The decoder is a mirror image of the encoder — four stride-2 transposed convolution layers expand 64×64 back to 512×512. Crucially, it is called only once at the very end of sampling, after all diffusion denoising steps are complete in latent space.

Decoder dimension flow $(4, 64, 64) \to (3, 512, 512)$
Called once after T denoising steps (not T times!)
Key efficiency insight
DDPM would call the decoder 1000 times in pixel space. Latent diffusion (SD 1.5, SDXL, AnimateDiff) calls the VAE decoder exactly once — only to produce the final output image. All 20–50 denoising steps happen in the compact 16k-value latent space.

Role in Every Model in This Course

  • Stable Diffusion 1.5 / SDXL: image VAE compresses 512×512 or 1024×1024 RGB to 64×64 or 128×128 latent before diffusion
  • CogVideoX / AnimateDiff: Temporal VAE extends to video, compressing (T, H, W) to (T/4, H/8, W/8) — 17M pixel values → under 1M latent values for a 49-frame clip
  • FLUX.1: uses the same KL-regularised VAE architecture but with 16 latent channels instead of 4 (higher information density for the transformer backbone)
