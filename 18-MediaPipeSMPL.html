MediaPipe → SMPL Bridge

MediaPipe outputs 33 screen-space 2D landmarks; SMPL requires 24 3D joints in a normalized T-pose space. This bridge applies Procrustes alignment and inverse-kinematics (θ regression) to close the gap.

33 → 24 Joint Mapping

MediaPipe Holistic outputs 33 pose landmarks indexed 0–32. SMPL has 24 joints with a different naming and hierarchy. Not all MediaPipe landmarks map to a SMPL joint — some are discarded and some SMPL joints (finger roots, jaw) have no MediaPipe equivalent.

02

Procrustes Alignment

MediaPipe landmarks are in pixel-space coordinates (0–1 normalised, depth from monocular estimate). SMPL joints live in a metric 3D space centred at the pelvis, scaled so shoulder-width ≈ 0.4 m. Procrustes alignment finds the optimal Rotation R, scale s, and translation t to align the two point sets.

Step 1 of 4

Procrustes Algorithm

Optimal rotation via SVD 1. Centre: X̄ = X - mean(X), Ȳ = Y - mean(Y)
2. Cross-covariance: H = X̄ᵀ Ȳ
3. SVD: H = U Σ Vᵀ
4. Rotation: R = V Uᵀ (det check for reflection)
5. Scale: s = trace(Σ) / trace(X̄ᵀX̄)
6. Translation: t = mean(Y) - s·R·mean(X)
03

Pose Regression (θ from Joints)

After Procrustes alignment we have SMPL joint positions in 3D. The final step recovers the SMPL pose parameters θ ∈ ℝ⁷² (24 joints × 3 axis-angle) via inverse kinematics. This is an optimisation problem — find θ such that the SMPL forward pass reproduces the observed joints.

IK Loss Optimisation
Adjust the regularisation weight λ and see how it balances joint accuracy vs. pose naturalness (prior). Lower λ = closer to observed joints, higher λ = more natural pose.

Optimisation Objective

IK loss function L(θ) = L_joints + λ·L_prior + μ·L_limit

L_joints = Σᵢ wᵢ ||J_pred(θ)ᵢ − J_obs,ᵢ||²
(joint position reconstruction error)

L_prior = ||θ||² (L2 regularisation → small rotations)

L_limit = Σⱼ max(0, θⱼ − θ_max,ⱼ)²
+ Σⱼ max(0, θ_min,ⱼ − θⱼ)²
(joint angle limit penalty)
Implementation approach
In practice this bridge is often replaced by a pretrained regressor such as HMR2 or ViTPose that directly outputs SMPL θ from a single image without iterative optimisation. The course uses the optimisation-based approach to build intuition for the loss landscape before introducing learned regressors.
