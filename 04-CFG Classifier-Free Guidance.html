Amplifying the Prompt

A step-by-step guide to how CFG steers diffusion models toward your text prompt — no separate classifier required.

01

The Core Formula

CFG runs the diffusion network twice at every denoising step — once with your text prompt, once without — then extrapolates beyond the conditioned direction using a guidance weight w.

One Network, Two Inference Runs

During training, the text conditioning is randomly dropped 10–20% of the time. This teaches the U-Net to denoise without a prompt as well as with one — so at inference we get two useful score estimates from a single model with no extra training.

Run 1 — Conditioned (with text prompt) ε_cond   = ε_θ(x_t, c)
Run 2 — Unconditioned (empty prompt) ε_uncond = ε_θ(x_t, ∅)

Combine with Guidance Weight w

The two estimates are combined so that the unconditioned direction is the baseline and the conditioned–unconditioned difference points "toward the prompt". Scaling that difference by w amplifies prompt adherence.

CFG Guided Score (used for the actual denoising step) ε̃ = ε_uncond + w · (ε_cond − ε_uncond)
  • w = 0: purely unconditioned — ignores prompt entirely
  • w = 1: exactly the conditioned estimate — no amplification
  • w = 7.5: amplified — the model commits harder to the prompt

Why Extrapolate Beyond w = 1?

A single denoising step provides only a weak nudge. Amplifying with w >1 makes the model commit more aggressively to the text condition at each step — at the cost of some sample diversity and eventually hallucinations at very high w.

Key insight
CFG is not a separate classifier. It's a mathematical trick using one network's own estimates to amplify the text signal — no extra parameters or training needed.

Reading the arrows

Grey = ε_uncond (no prompt)  ·  Teal = ε_cond (with prompt)  ·  Orange = ε̃ guided (actually used).
At w < 1 the guided arrow falls between grey and teal. At w > 1 it overshoots beyond teal — pushing harder toward the prompt region.

03

Choosing the Right w

The guidance scale w is the single most important inference hyperparameter. Too low = blurry images that ignore the prompt. Too high = over-saturated hallucinations.

CFG in Every Model in This Course

  • SDXL: default w = 7.5; dual text encoders (CLIP-L + OpenCLIP-G) produce a 2048-dim conditioning vector, giving CFG a richer signal to amplify
  • CogVideoX-2B: CFG guides every denoising step across all 49 frames — conditioning on both text prompt and optional image condition simultaneously
  • ControlNet: CFG is applied on top of the spatially conditioned score; the spatial control and text guidance stack additively
  • SANA-Sprint: the student distillation process bakes high-w CFG behaviour into a single-step model, eliminating the dual forward pass at inference
