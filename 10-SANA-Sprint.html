Knowledge Distillation for Diffusion

The standard diffusion sampling loop runs 20–50 steps to denoise from noise to image. Distillation trains a smaller/faster "student" to match the teacher's output distribution in far fewer steps.

How Distillation Works — Progressive Approach

Progressive distillation (Ho & Salimans 2022) trains a student to match the output of two teacher steps in a single student step. Repeat this: 50-step teacher → 25-step student → 12-step → 6-step → 1-step. At each round, the "teacher" is last round's student.

Distillation loss (simplified) L_distill = E[‖ x_student(x_t) − x_teacher(x_t→x_{t-2}) ‖²]

Consistency Models

Consistency models (Song et al. 2023) take a different approach: the model learns that all points on the same ODE trajectory must map to the same clean image. Once trained, a single forward pass from any noisy image x_t reaches the clean x_0.

Consistency property f_θ(x_t, t) = f_θ(x_t', t') for any t,t' on same ODE path
Inference: x_0 = f_θ(x_T, T) in ONE step
SANA-Sprint's lineage
SANA-Sprint combines the efficient transformer backbone from SANA (linear attention, AMP layers) with adversarial consistency distillation (ADD-style) and flow-matching training. The result: 1-step generation of 1024×1024 images in ~0.1 seconds on an H100 GPU.
02

Adversarial Consistency Distillation

SANA-Sprint uses a GAN discriminator alongside the consistency distillation objective. The discriminator forces the student to produce outputs that are perceptually indistinguishable from the teacher's 50-step results.

Two Loss Terms

The student is trained by two loss terms simultaneously:

  • Consistency loss: Student output f(x_t) must match teacher's multi-step output. Prevents mode collapse.
  • Adversarial loss: A discriminator D tries to distinguish student images from teacher images. The student minimises D's ability to do so. Forces high-frequency perceptual quality.
Combined objective L = λ_c · L_consistency + λ_adv · L_adversarial
L_adversarial = -E[log D(f_θ(x_t))] (student fools discriminator)

Why Speed Matters for This Course

In the full animation pipeline, diffusion runs per-frame. With 30 fps video, every second of output requires 30 individual inferences. At 1.8 s/frame (SD 1.5), 10 seconds of video takes 9 minutes. At 0.1 s/frame (SANA-Sprint), the same video takes 30 seconds. This unlocks iterative creative editing in near real time.

SANA linear attention
The speed gain is also architectural: SANA's backbone uses linear attention (O(N) instead of O(N²) per head) so the transformer scales to 1024×1024 without the quadratic cost hitting hard. SANA-Sprint inherits this efficient architecture and adds distillation on top.
