Three-Model Pipeline

Holistic is not one monolithic model — it's a pipeline of three specialized sub-models: BlazePose for body, a hand crop model for each hand, and MediaPipe FaceMesh for the face. They share a single forward pass by reusing intermediate feature maps.

BlazePose Backbone

BlazePose first runs a lightweight detector on the full frame to find a person bounding box. Then a keypoint model (MobileNet-based) returns 33 landmarks in 3D: (x, y, z, visibility). Z is relative depth in hip-width units — a rough depth estimate without stereo cameras.

BlazePose 33 joint output pose_landmarks[i] = (x_norm, y_norm, z_norm, visibility)
x,y ∈ [0,1] normalized to image dims
z: depth relative to hips, in hip-width units
visibility: sigmoid confidence [0,1]

Hand Crop and 21-Point Tracking

The wrist position from BlazePose is used to crop a 256×256 patch around each hand. A hand landmark model then returns 21 (x,y,z) landmarks per hand in that local crop, which Holistic projects back to global image coordinates. Left and right hands are tracked independently.

FaceMesh 468 Points

FaceMesh detects 468 face landmarks on a cropped face region. These cover eyes, iris, eyebrows, nose, lips, and face contour. The refined landmark variant also adds 5 iris landmarks per eye (478 total in newer versions). In Holistic, the face crop is derived from nose/ear landmarks detected by BlazePose.

Why 543, not 522?
33 pose + 21 left hand + 21 right hand + 468 face = 543. But if one or both hands are not visible, those landmarks are simply absent (or zero-filled). MediaPipe reports a multi_hand_landmarks list that may contain 0, 1, or 2 entries depending on hand detection confidence.
03

Output Format and Pipeline Usage

MediaPipe Holistic returns landmark data as normalised (x, y, z) coordinates plus a visibility/presence flag. In this course, Holistic output drives the SMPL pose fitting and ControlNet conditioning stages.

Coordinate System

All landmarks are in normalised image coordinates where (0,0) is top-left and (1,1) is bottom-right. For 3D-aware use you also have z, a relative depth estimate in body-scale units. Converting to pixel coordinates: just multiply by image width/height.

Coordinate conversion x_px = landmark.x * image_width
y_px = landmark.y * image_height
z_px ≈ landmark.z * image_width (depth in same scale as x)

To SMPL: 33 → 24 Joints

SMPL uses 24 body joints, while BlazePose provides 33. The mapping requires selecting corresponding joints and ignoring MediaPipe-specific landmarks (e.g., eye, ear) that don't have SMPL equivalents. Then Procrustes alignment scales and rotates the MediaPipe 33-point skeleton to best match the SMPL canonical T-pose joint positions before regressing SMPL pose parameters (θ ∈ ℝ⁷²).

Course pipeline context
In the talking-head animation pipeline: MediaPipe Holistic runs per-frame on a driving video → BlazePose 33 joints feed into SMPL pose regression → ControlNet receives the resulting skeleton overlay → CogVideoX / AnimateDiff generates the animated character. Face landmarks (468 pts) feed separately into the lip-sync pathway.
