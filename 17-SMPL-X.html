SMPL-X — Body, Hands & Face
in One Model

SMPL-X extends SMPL with detailed hand articulation via PCA finger poses and face expression control via 100-dimensional expression coefficients (ψ), all in a single differentiable model.

01

What SMPL-X Adds

SMPL-X ("eXpressive") extends SMPL (6890 vertices, 24 joints) to include fully articulated hands and facial expressions. The mesh grows to 10475 vertices and the joint set expands to 54 joints (24 body + 15 left hand + 15 right hand).

Joint Hierarchy Expansion

SMPL-X adds 30 joints for hands (15 per hand). Each finger has 3 knuckle joints (MCP, PIP, DIP) plus the fingertip. The thumb has 4 joints. These 15 joints × 3 axis-angle values = 45 values per hand, but SMPL-X compresses this via PCA — just 10 components per hand capture 98% of natural hand pose variation.

Hand pose compression $\theta_{\text{hand,raw}} \in \mathbb{R}^{45}$ (15 joints × 3 = 45 rot values)
$\theta_{\text{hand,pca}} \in \mathbb{R}^{10}$ (10 PCA components per hand)
$\theta_{\text{hand,raw}} = V_{\text{hand}} \cdot \theta_{\text{hand,pca}} + \mu_{\text{hand}}$
$V_{\text{hand}} \in \mathbb{R}^{45 \times 10}$ — PCA basis vectors from hand scans

Face Geometry — FLAME Head Model

The face region of SMPL-X incorporates vertices from the FLAME head model. The face has 5023 vertices. These are controlled by the same β shape coefficients (body shape affects face size), plus the jaw joint (θ_jaw ∈ ℝ³) and expression PCA coefficients ψ (see Chapter 2).

02

Expression Coefficients ψ

ψ ∈ ℝ¹⁰⁰ controls the facial expression of the SMPL-X head. Each component adds a blend shape displacement to face vertices — similar to how ARKit blendshapes work but in a linear PCA space rather than named semantic shapes.

Expression Morph Viewer
Drag sliders to see how the first two expression PCA components affect a simplified face outline. ψ₀ ≈ intensity/openness, ψ₁ ≈ asymmetric stretch.

ψ vs ARKit Blendshapes

ARKit (and Metahuman) uses 52 named blendshapes (jawOpen, eyeBlinkLeft, etc.) specifically designed for face rendering pipelines. SMPL-X's ψ is a lower-level statistical representation from 3D scan PCA and is not named semantically. In this course's pipeline, we convert SMPL-X ψ → ARKit blendshapes via a learned regression (App 25: Viseme→Blendshapes).

Expression blend shapes $\text{face\_verts\_expr} = \text{face\_verts} + \sum_i \psi_i \cdot E_i$
$E_i \in \mathbb{R}^{5023 \times 3}$ — i-th expression basis (per face vertex)
$\psi \in \mathbb{R}^{100}$ — expression PCA coefficients

SMPL vs SMPL-X Comparison

Choosing between SMPL and SMPL-X depends on whether you need detailed hand or face expression control. SMPL is faster and simpler; SMPL-X is more expressive but has more parameters to optimize and a more complex mesh.

Course usage context
SMPL-X is used in this course for the full talking-head pipeline: β+θ drives body pose for ControlNet conditioning, θ_jaw drives lip open/close during speech, and ψ drives the 100-dim expression coefficients that are converted to ARKit blendshapes for the final face renderer. The 10-dim hand PCA is typically ignored (set to zero) for face-only clips.
