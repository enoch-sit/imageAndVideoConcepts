SMPL — Skinned Multi-Person
Linear Body Model

How SMPL represents any human body with just 82 numbers: 10 shape parameters (β) controlling body size/proportions and 72 pose parameters (θ) controlling joint rotations.

β ∈ ℝ¹⁰ Shape θ ∈ ℝ⁷² Pose 6890 Mesh Vertices
01

Shape Space — β Parameters

β is a 10-dimensional vector that captures body shape variation across a population. It is derived from PCA on a dataset of thousands of 3D body scans. β=0 gives the mean body; positive/negative values move along principal variation axes (height, weight, limb proportions, etc.).

Shape Morph Explorer
Drag sliders to change the first two shape principal components. The silhouette shows the resulting body shape change relative to the neutral SMPL template.

Shape as PCA Coefficients

SMPL's shape blend shapes are a set of 10 orthonormal basis vectors (each 6890×3 dimensional — one displacement vector per vertex). The β coefficients weight these bases:

Shape blend shape formula T_shaped = T_mean + Σᵢ βᵢ · Sᵢ

T_mean ∈ ℝ^(6890×3) — mean body template
Sᵢ ∈ ℝ^(6890×3) — i-th shape basis vector
β ∈ ℝ¹⁰ — shape coefficients
02

Pose Space — θ Parameters

θ ∈ ℝ⁷² encodes joint rotations as axis-angle vectors for 24 body joints (including the root). Each joint gets a 3D axis-angle vector (3 × 24 = 72 values). Axis-angle (r) is compact: the direction of r is the rotation axis and |r| is the angle in radians.

24 Joint Hierarchy

The 24 SMPL joints are organized in a kinematic tree from the pelvis root. Child joints inherit parent transforms. The 24 joints cover: pelvis (0), left/right legs (1-6), spine chain (6-9), shoulders/elbows/wrists (13-17,10-12), and hands (18-23).

03

Linear Blend Skinning (LBS)

Given shape (β) and pose (θ), SMPL produces a 3D mesh via Linear Blend Skinning: weighted blending of the 6890 vertices according to which joints they are "attached to" (skinning weights W).

The SMPL LBS Formula

Every vertex v is transformed by a weighted average of joint transformation matrices, where the weights W ∈ ℝ^(6890×24) are fixed (learned from data). High weight near a joint = that joint dominates the vertex position.

Full SMPL forward pass T(β) = T_mean + Σ βᵢ Sᵢ + Σ B_p(θ) (pose blends)
vᵢ' = (Σⱼ wᵢⱼ · Gⱼ(θ,J(β))) · vᵢ

wᵢⱼ = skinning weight of vertex i for joint j
Gⱼ = world transformation matrix of joint j
J(β) = joint positions derived from shape β
