Viseme Mapping

A viseme is the smallest visual unit of speech — the lip shape seen during a phoneme. Many distinct phonemes share the same lip shape and thus the same viseme. The 39 ARPAbet phonemes collapse to just 15 viseme classes, dramatically reducing the blendshape complexity needed for lip sync.

01

15 English Viseme Classes

The 15-viseme system (Preston & Yuen, standardised in ARKit/MPEG-4) groups phonemes by the lip/jaw/tongue visible configuration. Viseme 0 is the silent/rest position. Click any viseme row to see which ARPAbet phonemes it contains and a mouth-shape reference.

Why so few visemes?

Human vision has low acuity for distinguishing the subtle articulatory differences between, say, /b/, /p/ and /m/ — all require bilabial closure and look identical on video. Similarly, velar stops /g/ and /k/ happen entirely at the back of the throat — invisible. This perceptual collapse means 15 viseme shapes are sufficient to generate convincing lip sync for an English speaker.

Python implementation P2V = {

"F":"ff", "V":"ff", # viseme 8 (labiodental)
"TH":"TH","DH":"TH", # viseme 9 (interdental)

}
def phoneme_to_viseme(ph): return P2V.get(ph.upper(), "sil")

Timing and Coarticulation

Direct viseme switching at phoneme boundaries looks robotic. Coarticulation is the phenomenon where the mouth begins to transition toward the next viseme while still producing the current phoneme. We model this with a blending window.

Coarticulation Blend Slider
Drag the blend slider to see how the transitions between visemes become smooth (or abrupt) depending on the anticipatory blend window.
30ms

Anticipatory and Carry-over Coarticulation

The blend formula uses a half-cosine window centred at the phoneme boundary. Anticipatory coarticulation starts transitioning before the boundary (the current phoneme starts blending toward the next); carry-over coarticulation continues into the start of the next phoneme.

Viseme blending formula w(t) = ½ · (1 - cos(π · t / T_blend))
v_blend(t) = (1 - w(t)) · v_current + w(t) · v_next
where t ∈ [-T_blend/2, T_blend/2] around boundary
T_blend ≈ 30ms (default), max = 50% of shorter phoneme
Output: Timed Viseme Sequence
The final output of this stage is a time series of (viseme_id, weight, timestamp) events. This is passed directly to App 25 (Viseme→Blendshapes), which maps each viseme_id to the set of ARKit blendshape weights needed to achieve that mouth shape in the 3D avatar.
