Encoder Architecture

The Temporal VAE's encoder processes a raw video tensor (T, C, H, W) through 3D convolutions that jointly reduce spatial and temporal dimensions, producing a compressed latent that the diffusion transformer operates on.

Stage 1 of 5

3D vs 2D Convolution

Standard 2D VAE (used in Stable Diffusion) applies 2D convolutions independently to each frame. This compresses spatial dimensions (H/8, W/8) but leaves temporal dimension unchanged — all frames remain independent. The Temporal VAE adds 3D tconv kernels with temporal stride, so the encoder also compresses across frames, resulting in T/4.

Compression dimensions 2D VAE input: (T, 3, H, W)
2D VAE output: (T, 4, H/8, W/8) ← frames independent
Temporal VAE output: (T/4, 4, H/8, W/8) ← jointly compressed

Latent Dimensions for CogVideoX

For 49 frames at 480×720: the Temporal VAE produces a latent of shape (13, 4, 60, 90). This 4-channel 3D tensor is what the CogVideoX DiT transformer operates on — roughly 28000 video tokens (vs 4M raw pixels per second).

Shape arithmetic T_out = (T-1)/4 + 1 = (49-1)/4+1 = 13 (causal first frame)
H_out = 480/8 = 60
W_out = 720/8 = 90
Latent tokens = 13 × 60 × 90 = 70,200

Causal 3D Convolutions

A causal convolution at time step t can only look at frames t, t−1, t−2, ... — never future frames. This maintains temporal order: the model can generate one frame at a time in an autoregressive manner without recomputing the entire sequence.

Padding Strategy

To implement causal behavior, the Temporal VAE pads the time dimension on the left only. A temporal kernel of size k=3 pads by k−1=2 zeros at the start. This ensures the convolution always looks at present and past frames, never future frames.

Causal padding equation pad = (kernel_t - 1, 0) on time axis
For kernel_t=3: pad (2,0) → output[t] depends on input[t-2:t+1]
For first frame: padded zeros act as "empty past"
Why Causal Matters for Generation
Non-causal 3D convolutions require the full video to be available before encoding begins. Causal convolutions allow the decoder to reconstruct frames one by one as they are denoised. CogVideoX uses this to enable streaming decode: show frame 0, then frame 1, etc., as generation finishes.
03

Compression Ratio Comparison

The quality vs compression tradeoff of the Temporal VAE is critical. Too much compression degrades temporal detail; too little makes the diffusion problem too expensive. The 4× temporal + 8× spatial factor was tuned for quality at 6-second clips.
